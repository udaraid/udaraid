Past Projects

Highlights from the UDAR AID Anniversary Report.

Emergency Medical Attention for the Injured

  • Your support rushed life‑saving care to revolution survivors—covering surgeries, medications, and recovery costs when families had nowhere else to turn.
  • 292 patients documented
  • 280 received direct financial support
  • Comprehensive case tracking and follow‑up

Long‑Term Rehabilitation Support

  • Monthly sponsorship helped injured families regain stability—covering food, rent, treatment, and children’s education while they rebuilt their lives.
  • BDT 10,000+ per family monthly
  • 6‑month commitment (Jan–Jun 2025)
  • Families: Manik & Faruk, Nupur, Mamun, Mufti Firoz

Flood Fund Relief

  • In the aftermath of the August 2024 floods, your generosity delivered emergency relief packages and restored hope to devastated families.
  • 300+ families received emergency relief
  • Food, hygiene kits, clean water, shelter materials
  • One destroyed home rebuilt

Ramadan & Eid Campaigns

  • You helped families observe sacred days with dignity through iftar meals, groceries, and Eid support for those most in need.
  • $1,425 total distributed
  • 200+ families benefited
  • 100+ orphan children supported

Individuals in Crisis

  • When emergencies struck, donors stepped in—covering urgent medical bills, disability support, and livelihood restoration for vulnerable families.
  • Helal’s Campaign: $1,085 raised and distributed
  • Mr Motiur: $100 support sent in March
  • Abdul Kader: $328 (BDT 40,000) for a Mishuk
  • Shahbuddin: family support after severe injuries
  • Cancer chemotherapy survivor: first session covered ($350)
  • Nur Mohammad: paralysis support case

Madrassa Project

  • Supported a remote village madrassa in Kishoreganj (Itna Upazilla) to build a permanent school structure for 70+ students from low‑income families.
  • Fundraising canvas session in New York helped raise and send 98,289 BDT for construction.
  • Construction is in full swing; total project need is approximately 3,20,000 BDT.
  • Students now have a safer, more permanent learning space, especially during monsoon season.
